What makes up your credit score?

35% Payment History

The most important factor. Pay every bill on time, every month. Even one late payment can hurt your score significantly.

30% Amounts Owed

How much of your available credit you're using. Try to stay below 30% of your limit. Example: if your card limit is $1,000, keep your balance under $300.

15% Length of History

How long you've had credit. The longer, the better. This is why you should open your first card as soon as possible and never close it.

10% New Credit

Every time you apply for a new credit card or loan, your score dips slightly. Don't apply for too many things at once.

10% Credit Mix

Having different types of credit (a credit card, a car loan, etc.) helps your score. But don't take on debt just for this — it's the least important factor.
